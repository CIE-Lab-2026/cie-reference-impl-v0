# CIE Governance Model

This document describes the governance model of
Collective Intelligence Engineering (CIE).

Governance stabilizes evolving meaning structures
while preserving adaptability across the intelligence system.

---

## Governance Principle

Traditional governance controls behavior.

CIE governance controls meaning evolution.

The objective is not restriction,
but maintaining coherence, traceability, and alignment
across evolving knowledge systems.

---

## Why Governance Matters

In evolving intelligence environments:

- meanings shift
- interpretations diverge
- structures reorganize
- assumptions change

Without governance, evolution becomes fragmentation.

Governance enables sustainable intelligence growth.

---

## Governance Scope

CIE governance operates across:

Meaning Structures  
Knowledge Graph Evolution  
Ontology Stability  
Decision Context Alignment  
Agent Interaction Boundaries

Together, these define the stability envelope of collective intelligence.

---

## Meaning Governance

Meaning is dynamic but must remain interpretable.

Meaning governance ensures:

- semantic continuity
- interpretability across iterations
- traceable conceptual transitions

This allows knowledge to evolve without losing structure.

---

## Knowledge Graph Governance

Knowledge structures continuously expand.

Governance maintains:

- structural integrity
- relationship consistency
- evolution traceability

This prevents uncontrolled semantic drift.

---

## Ontology Governance

Ontologies evolve through interaction.

Governance ensures:

- concept stability across contexts
- compatibility between agents
- controlled semantic transformation

This supports Ontology in the Loop Systems (OiLS).

---

## Decision Context Governance

Collective intelligence depends on shared assumptions.

Governance aligns:

- evaluation criteria
- reasoning boundaries
- interpretation frameworks
- action constraints

This enables reliable coordination across participants.

---

## Agent Boundary Governance

Participants operate with different:

- goals
- interpretations
- knowledge states
- authority levels

Governance defines safe interaction boundaries
without limiting constructive evolution.

---

## Evolutionary Governance

CIE governance does not freeze meaning.

Instead, it regulates:

- transition speed
- structural compatibility
- semantic continuity

Governance enables evolution without instability.

---

## Governance vs Control

Control restricts change.

Governance shapes change.

CIE supports adaptive stability instead of rigid enforcement.

---

## Recursive Governance

Governance itself evolves.

Each iteration refines:

- alignment criteria
- stability thresholds
- semantic compatibility rules

This allows governance to scale with intelligence complexity.

---

## Summary

Governance stabilizes collective intelligence
while preserving meaning evolution.

CIE governs structure without suppressing emergence.
