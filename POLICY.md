# CIE Public Disclosure Policy (Personal Research)

This document defines the disclosure policy for
CIE (Collective Intelligence Engineering) and
OiLS (Ontology in the Loop Systems)
as an independent personal conceptual exploration.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to clarify the scope,
intent, and boundaries of public materials related to CIE / OiLS.

This project explores how collective intelligence can be designed
as an evolving system through human–AI interaction.

---

## Independence Statement

CIE / OiLS is an independent conceptual research activity.

It is not derived from organizational work,
internal architecture, or proprietary engineering processes.

All materials published under this repository reflect
personal exploration only.

---

## Scope of Public Content

The following categories are intentionally shared:

### Conceptual Structures

Examples:

- Collective Intelligence Engineering (CIE)
- Ontology in the Loop Systems (OiLS)
- Problem Cell
- Knowledge Graph evolution models
- Intelligence lifecycle concepts

### Abstract Models

Examples:

- synchronization structures
- governance layers
- ontology evolution frameworks
- interaction persistence models

### Design Philosophy

Examples:

- intelligence as infrastructure
- dialogue as an evolving system
- knowledge as a persistent structure

---

## Non-Public Content

The following categories are intentionally excluded:

### Organizational Architectures

Including:

- internal workflows
- validation pipelines
- toolchains
- deployment structures

### Customer or Industry Applications

Including:

- case studies
- metrics
- evaluation frameworks
- applied integration examples

### Implementation-Level Specifications

Including:

- executable system structures
- integration blueprints
- engineering deployment patterns

---

## Boundary Rule

Before publishing new material, the following questions apply:

1. Was this created inside organizational activity?
2. Is this currently used in an organizational workflow?
3. Is this planned for organizational implementation?

If any answer is "Yes",
the material is not included in this repository.

---

## Disclosure Principle

CIE / OiLS focuses on:

meaning structures rather than implementation structures.

Conceptual architectures are shared.
Operational architectures are intentionally excluded.

---

## Future Alignment Possibility

This work is currently independent.

Future alignment with organizations,
research communities, or engineering frameworks
may be explored separately if appropriate.

---

## Status

Early conceptual exploration phase.
Structure and terminology may evolve over time.
