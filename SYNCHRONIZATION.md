# CIE Synchronization Model

This document describes the synchronization model of
Collective Intelligence Engineering (CIE).

Synchronization enables multiple agents, knowledge structures,
and meaning systems to evolve coherently over time.

---

## Synchronization Principle

Traditional systems synchronize data states.

CIE synchronizes meaning states.

The goal of synchronization is not uniformity,
but structural coherence across evolving intelligence layers.

---

## Why Synchronization Matters

In evolving intelligence systems:

- meaning shifts
- context changes
- interpretations diverge
- knowledge structures reorganize

Without synchronization, collective intelligence fragments.

Synchronization maintains continuity.

---

## Synchronization Targets

CIE synchronizes across multiple dimensions:

Agent Synchronization  
Knowledge Synchronization  
Ontology Synchronization  
Decision Context Synchronization  
Temporal Synchronization

Together, these maintain system-wide coherence.

---

## Agent Synchronization

Agent synchronization aligns:

- interpretations
- intent
- reasoning direction
- semantic expectations

This allows multiple participants to operate within
compatible meaning structures.

---

## Knowledge Synchronization

Knowledge synchronization maintains consistency across:

- Problem Cells
- Knowledge Graph structures
- evolution pathways

It prevents structural drift during knowledge growth.

---

## Ontology Synchronization (OiLS)

Ontology evolves during interaction.

Synchronization ensures that:

- concepts remain interpretable
- definitions remain stable
- semantic drift remains traceable

Ontology is continuously updated inside the loop.

This defines the Ontology in the Loop Systems (OiLS) model.

---

## Decision Context Synchronization

Decision environments depend on shared meaning.

Synchronization aligns:

- assumptions
- evaluation criteria
- reasoning boundaries
- action constraints

This enables coordinated decision-making.

---

## Temporal Synchronization

Collective intelligence evolves over time.

Temporal synchronization maintains alignment between:

- past knowledge states
- present interpretations
- future evolution directions

This creates continuity across iterations.

---

## Synchronization Across Layers

Synchronization operates across all CIE layers:

Interaction Layer  
Structuring Layer  
Persistence Layer  
Evolution Layer  
Governance Layer

Each layer contributes to system-wide coherence.

---

## Synchronization vs Standardization

Standardization enforces sameness.

Synchronization enables compatibility.

CIE supports evolving diversity within shared structure.

---

## Recursive Synchronization

Synchronization itself evolves.

Each iteration refines:

- meaning alignment
- ontology stability
- structural compatibility

This allows the system to scale without fragmentation.

---

## Summary

Synchronization is the mechanism that allows
collective intelligence to remain coherent while evolving.

CIE does not enforce uniform meaning.

CIE enables aligned meaning evolution.
